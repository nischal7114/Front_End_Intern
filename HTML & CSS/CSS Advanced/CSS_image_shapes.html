Kendrick Lamar Duckworth (born June 17, 1987) is an American rapper. Regarded as one of the greatest rappers of all time, he was awarded the 2018 Pulitzer Prize for Music, becoming the first musician outside of the classical and jazz genres to receive the honor. Kendrick Lamar gave a captivating performance at the Super Bowl LIX halftime show in New Orleans on February 9. Fresh off his Grammy wins the week prior, the rapper made history as the first solo hip-hop artist to headline the Super Bowl as he played a medley of his popular songs at the Caesars Superdome.
